Ocean's Silent Cry

Millions of tons of plastic flood the oceans annually, mainly through rivers, escalating a crisis where only 9% of the 8.3 billion tonnes of plastic produced has been recycled. With 150 million tonnes already contaminating oceans and an additional 12 million entering yearly, urgent action is imperative.

Current removal methods, notably manual extraction from rivers, are ineffective, capturing less than 1% and proving economically unviable. Without significant interventions, projections indicate a tripling of plastic pollution by 2030, casting a bleak future for both oceans and humanity.[…]

  • Marine Menace: Plastic Pervasion

    Over 8 million tons of plastic find their way into the ocean each year, posing a dire threat to marine life and ecosystems.

  • Toxic Tides: Chemical Contamination

    Plastic waste in the ocean absorbs and accumulates harmful chemicals, impacting fish and other marine species.

  • Invisible Chains: Entanglement and Debris

    An estimated 1 million seabirds and 100,000 marine mammals are killed annually due to plastic entanglement or ingestion.

  • Microplastic Peril: A Silent Threat

    Up to 11,000 plastic particles infiltrate the ocean's food chain annually, a potential threat to both marine life and unwitting seafood consumers.
